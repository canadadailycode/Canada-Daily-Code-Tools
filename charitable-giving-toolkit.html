Charitable donation tax credit calculator

Federal rate: 14% on the first $200 of annual donations, 29% above $200 (33% on the portion of donations claimable against income taxed in the top federal bracket, $258,482+ in 2026). Provincial rates below are the official 2026 published rates for each province/territory. No. 02

Multi-year bunching comparison

The CRA lets you carry forward unclaimed donations for up to 5 years and claim them together in one tax year. Because the first $200 of a claim gets the lower rate, combining several years' small donations into one claim can produce a larger total credit than claiming small amounts each year. Enter your province and a few years of donation amounts to compare.
No. 03

Donating securities vs. selling and donating cash

Under the Income Tax Act, donating publicly listed securities (shares, ETFs, mutual fund units) directly to a registered charity carries a 0% capital gains inclusion rate — you owe no capital gains tax on the appreciation, and you still receive a donation receipt for the full fair market value. If you sell first and donate the cash instead, the capital gain is taxed normally before the donation credit applies. This calculator shows the difference — enter your own estimated marginal tax rate on capital gains, since that depends on your total income and province.

Why this matters

  • Both methods give you the same donation tax credit, based on the fair market value of the gift.
  • Only the "donate securities directly" method avoids capital gains tax on the built-up appreciation — but the shares must transfer in-kind directly from your brokerage to the charity's brokerage account. Selling first and donating the cash proceeds does not qualify.
  • This calculator uses the marginal tax rate you provide — it does not calculate your personal tax bracket, since that depends on your total income, province, and other factors your accountant should confirm.
