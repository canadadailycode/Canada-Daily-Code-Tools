What this number does — and doesn't — tell you

What a lower CPP contribution means

Paying yourself less in T4 salary (and more in dividends instead) reduces or eliminates CPP contributions, which is real, immediate cash-flow savings. But that's only one side of the ledger:

  • Dividends aren't free of tax — they're taxed personally at dividend tax rates (which vary by province) after the corporation has already paid corporate tax on that income. The combined personal + corporate tax rate on dividends can be higher or lower than the tax on an equivalent salary, depending on your province and income level — this calculator does not model that comparison.
  • Skipping CPP contributions reduces your future CPP retirement pension and your eligibility for CPP disability and survivor benefits — CPP isn't just a cost, it's a benefit you're opting out of.
  • Salary (not dividends) creates RRSP contribution room and counts as earned income for other purposes (like childcare deduction limits).
  • A "reasonable salary" may be required for other reasons — for example, some provincial or federal programs and lending decisions look at T4 income specifically.

In short: this tool tells you the CPP cost difference precisely. It cannot tell you whether salary or dividends is better for your business overall — that's a personalized calculation your accountant should run with your specific numbers.

How your data is stored: nothing you enter here is saved, sent anywhere, or tracked. This page runs entirely offline in your browser with no analytics and no network calls.
